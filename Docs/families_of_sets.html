1. Subtopic of

Sets

2. Definitions

Let A be a non empty set, and for each \(\alpha \in A\), \(A_\alpha\) be a set. then \(\{ A_\alpha; \alpha \in A \}\) is called a family of sets, and A is an index set for this family.

To generalizing the above concepts we define intersectin and union on family of sets:

Let X be a set, and \(\mathbb{A} := \{ A_\alpha; \alpha \in A \}\) be family of subsets of X.

  • \(\bigcap_\alpha A_\alpha := \{ x \in X; \forall \alpha \in A: x \in A_\alpha \}\)
  • \(\bigcup_\alpha A_\alpha := \{ x \in X; \exists \alpha \in A: x \in A_\alpha \}\)

3. Propositions

Let \(\{ A_\alpha; \alpha \in A \}\) and \(\{ B_\beta; \beta \in B \}\) be family of subsets of X.

3.1. Associativity

  • \((\bigcap_\alpha A_\alpha) \cap (\bigcap_\beta B_\beta) = \bigcap_{(\alpha,\beta)} A_\alpha \cap B_\beta\)
  • \((\bigcup_\alpha A_\alpha) \cup (\bigcup_\beta B_\beta) = \bigcup_{(\alpha,\beta)} A_\alpha \cup B_\beta\)

3.2. Distributivity

  • \((\bigcap_\alpha A_\alpha) \cup (\bigcap_\beta B_\beta) = \bigcap_{(\alpha,\beta)} A_\alpha \cup B_\beta\)
  • \((\bigcup_\alpha A_\alpha) \cap (\bigcup_\beta B_\beta) = \bigcup_{(\alpha,\beta)} A_\alpha \cap B_\beta\)

3.3. de Morgan’s laws

  • \((\bigcap_\alpha A_\alpha)^c = \bigcup_\alpha A_\alpha^c\)
  • \((\bigcup_\alpha A_\alpha)^c = \bigcap_\alpha A_\alpha^c\)
