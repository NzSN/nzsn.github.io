1. Introduction

Temporal Prediction is a technique that use to do inter-compression in H.264.

Prediction is describe by predicted frame which is created from one or more past or future frames known as reference frame.

2. Terminology

  • reference frames: Frames that used to generate predicted frame for current frame. It may exists at past or future.
  • Macroblock: 16x16-pixel region of a frame.

3.2. Methods to predict:

3.2.1. Optical flow of every pixel

Estimate the trajectory of each pixel between successive video frames.

  1. Disadvantage

    But this methods is not a practical method cause optical flow vector for every pixel is too big to transfered.

3.2.2. Block-based motion estimation and compensation

Assume that you are attempt to do motion estimation and compensation for a MxN samples then.

Following subseciton is under the assumption that show above.

  1. Motion Estimation

    A process to search an area, the area is the centerd on the current block postion, in the reference frame to find a similar MxN-sample region.

    Matching criterion is the lower energy in the residual the better.

  2. Motion Compensation

    The chosen candidate region as a predictor for the current MxN block and is subtracted from the current block to form a residual block (motion compensation).

    The residual block is encoded and transmited and the motion vector, the offset between current block and the position of predictor, is also transmited.

  3. Advantage
    • Fit well with rectangular video frames
    • Provide reasonably effective temporal model with block-based image transforms.
  4. Disadvantage
    • Real objects rarely have neat edge that match rectangular boundaries, this cause hard to compensate motion of such objects.

3.2.3. Motion compensated prediction of a macroblock

  1. Motion Estimation

    Similar to Block-based but the search area is 16x16-sample region.

  2. Motion Compensation
