1. Overview

This document is about basic struct of Linear algebra, Vector.

For example:

\(\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{w} = \begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} d \\ e \\ f \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a + d \\ b + e \\ c + f \end{bmatrix}\)

2. Operations

2.1. Vector Addition

Suppose there are two vectors:

\(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{w} = \begin{bmatrix} d \\ e \\ f \end{bmatrix}\)

then vector addition of these two vectors is

\(\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{w} = \begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} d \\ e \\ f \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a + d \\ b + e \\ c + f \end{bmatrix}\)

2.2. Scalar Multiplication

Multiply every component of a vector by a constant.

\(2\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 2v_{1} \\ 2v_{2} \\ 2v_{3} \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{v} + \mathbf{v}\)

With Vector Addition and Scalar Multiplication you are able to generate Linear Combination.

2.3. Dot Product

Suppose there are two vectors

\(\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{w} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix}\)

then their dot product is

\(\mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{w} = (1)(4) + (2)(5) = 4 + 10 = 14\)

Square root of \(\mathbf{v} * \mathbf{v}\) is the length of vector \(\mathbf{v}\), \(\parallel\mathbf{v}\parallel\). When you do a dot product

\(length = \parallel v \parallel = \sqrt{v \cdot v} = (v^{2}_{1} + v^{2}_{2} + ... + v^{2}_{n})^{\frac{1}{2}\)

First vector of dot product use row form:

Instead of \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\) we more often use \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\) or \(\mathbf{v^{'}} * \mathbf{w}\)

3. Lengths and Angle

3.1. Formula

  • (COSINE FORMULA) \(cos\theta = \frac{\mathbf{v}*\mathbf{w}}{\parallel v \parallel \parallel w \parallel}\)
  • (SCHWARZ INEQUALITY) \(|v \cdot w| \leq \parallel v \parallel \parallel w \parallel\)
  • (TRIANGLE INEQUALITY) \(\parallel v + w \parallel \leq \parallel v \parallel + \parallel w \parallel\)

The angle \(\theta\) between \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) has \(cos\theta = \frac{\mathbf{v}*\mathbf{w}}{\parallel v \parallel \parallel w \parallel}\) (COSINE FORMULA).

Fomr this formula we can have corollary

  • Dot product is zero if two vectors are perpendicular.

4. Special Vector

4.1. Unit Vector

A vector whose length equals one. Then \(u \cdot u = 1\).

4.2. Zero Vector

A vector whose length equals zero.
