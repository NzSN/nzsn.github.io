1. Spaces of Vectors

A Vector Space contains all the vectors that follow a specific rule. For example, \(\mathbb{R}^{3}\) contains all vectors with three real components.

A real vector space is a set of “vectors” together with rules for vector addition and for multiplication by real numbers.

At some times, vector can be a function, a matrix or something another things but at most of times we refer it as a ordinary column vectors.

1.1. Subspace

A subspace \(S\) of a vector space is a set of vectors (including 0) that satisfies two requirements: If v and w are vectors in the subspace and c is any scalar, then

  1. \(\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{w} \in \mathbb{S}\)
  2. \(c\mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{S}\)

In another word, \(\mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w} \in S \Rightarrow \text{Combination of } \mathbf{v} \text{ and } \mathbf{w} \in S\)

Definition of Subspace require operations must be closed.

2. The Column Space of A

In short, a column space of A is all the combinations of columns of A, writed as \(\mathbf{C}(A)\).

In another word, \(\mathbf{C}(\mathbb{A})\) is a vector space made up of columns of Matrix.

Base on this definition we have proposition:

  • \(\mathbb{A}\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}\) is solvable if and only if b is in the column space of \(\mathbb{A}\).

3. The Nullspace of A

The nullspace \(N(A)\) consists of all solutions to \(\mathbb{A}\mathbf{x}= \mathbf{0}\). These vectors \(\mathbf{x}\) are in \(\mathbb{R}^{n}\).
